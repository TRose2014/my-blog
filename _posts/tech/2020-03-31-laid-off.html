Back Story

In case you haven’t been following up with my story, I just wanted to provide a quick run-down on where I was before I wrote this post. So after I did my 5 months of classroom training, I started my on-the-job training in August 2019 as a Jr. Software Developer. I was exposed to the world of AWS, got my AWS Solutions Architect – Associate Certification, fell in love with Python, and so much more. Unfortunately, that all came crashing down, about a week ago when I got the call from my boss that me as well as my other coworkers who went through the same training were laid off.

What do I do now?

At first I was heartbroken. Was it something I could have prevented? Was I not good enough? But my company assured me was strictly because of COVID-19, and that they were hoping it would be just a temporary lay-off. So after a day of feeling sorry for myself, I had to figure out what my next steps were going to be. Obviously I had to keep coding, I came this far, to just give it up now because I was being lazy is definitely not an option. But the glaring problem was job hunting. It seems that every job I looked at, wants 3-5+ years’ experience and a CS degree. How am I going to overcome that? Although the job hunt seems daunting, I can’t quit. I can only control my actions and that is what I plan to do. So what is my game plan?

Game Plan

If you know me, you know I am pretty organized and always have a game plan. Whether that be when I am coding on a project or in life. I write out my steps on how I plan to tackle something and follow through. I realized this has made me a very productive developer when I write out my ideas and thoughts. So here are my steps on overcoming being out of work.

  1. Code every day
    • While I was in my classroom training, we had code challenges. So I plan on re-doing those in JavaScript and also again in Python. I also have a few ideas for some side projects I want to get started on
  2. Beef up my LinkedIn profile
  3. Make an excel sheet and list all the jobs I have applied to and when so I don’t get confused
  4. Look at other sites that post jobs such as Tech Ladies, Stack Overflow, etc.
  5. Since I don’t have a CS degree, I want to get a better understanding of CS and programming concepts: Big O, hoisting, contextual this, etc.

Conclusion

These are things I can control, and hopefully I can find a company that understands the importance of Jr. Developers and can see that I can bring much more to the table than just coding skills.
